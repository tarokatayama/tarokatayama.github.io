## Science Communication

One of the main reasons why I chose to go back to grad school and choose the Nicholas school in particular was because it was a professional masters that emphasized science communication. The Nicholas School taught me tools to translate a lot of nuanced sciences in a more digestible way. I was able to strengthen my abilities to communicate more effectively through brief memos, using data visualization, and working collaboratively.

Currently I am a science communication fellow for the National Park Service at the Pacific West Regional office. One of my personal goals of this fellowship is to be able to help make science more accessible, fun, and engaging. Through this fellowship, I have helped create a science communication ambassadorship where likeminded people in the NPS come together to discuss the direction of science communication within the Pacific West Region. One of the main goals of this ambassadorship is to make science communication more accessible to scientists in parks. Our hopes are to be able to assist these park scientists in creating unified, coherent, and engaging science communication pieces.
